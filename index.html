Why Calculus Matters in Animal Husbandry Tech

In Animal Husbandry (Program SFH), weight gain cannot be assessed as simple averages. Traditional livestock managers only compute Average Daily Gain (ADG) backwards using discrete math:

ADG_average = [Weight(Day 42) - Weight(Day 0)] / 42

This crude measurement fails to recognize that on Day 5, a chick gains roughly 15g/day, while on Day 28, it reaches its dynamic physiological peak, gaining up to 100g/day. Calculus provides the instantaneous rate of gain $f'(x)$, identifying critical physiological growth shifts.

Key Management Decisions Derived from $f'(x)$:
  • Phase Feeding Adjustments: Transitioning feed types (Starter to Grower, Grower to Finisher) right as rate acceleration slows down.
  • Harvest Scheduling: Determining peak metabolic efficiency before feed conversion decays.

Deriving the Growth Rate Function f'(x)

To calculate the instantaneous rate of animal weight change, we apply basic calculus rules (specifically the Quotient Rule and Chain Rule) to the Logistic Growth equation:

Let the cumulative broiler weight function be represented as:
f(x) = L * (1 + a * e^{-k * x})^{-1}
Where a is a constant related to initial chick weight (Day 0 hatch weight).
Applying the Chain Rule ($\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{dy}{du} \cdot \frac{du}{dx}$) where $u(x) = 1 + a \cdot e^{-k \cdot x}$:
f'(x) = -L * (1 + a * e^{-k * x})^{-2} * \frac{d}{dx}[1 + a * e^{-k * x}]
Taking the derivative of the inner function, $\frac{d}{dx}[1 + a \cdot e^{-k \cdot x}] = -k \cdot a \cdot e^{-k \cdot x}$:
f'(x) = -L * (1 + a * e^{-k * x})^{-2} * (-k * a * e^{-k * x})
Simplifying the expressions yields the final diagnostic growth-rate derivative formula:
f'(x) = \frac{L * k * a * e^{-k * x}}{(1 + a * e^{-k * x})^2}
This represents the **Average Daily Gain (ADG)** at any given day. Note how the maximum value of $f'(x)$ is achieved exactly at the inflection point $x = \frac{\ln(a)}{k}$.
